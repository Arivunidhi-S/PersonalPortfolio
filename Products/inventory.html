Inventory System PPEI – Modules & Features

TNB Janamanjung Sdn Bhd was incorporated in 1996. The company generates electricity from a coal-fired power plant and operates as a subsidiary of Tenaga Nasional Berhad.

For Tenaga Manjung (TNB), safety equipment includes standard PPE such as insulated gloves, non-conductive tools, safety helmets (often color-coded), and high-visibility vests, as well as specialized gear for electrical work and power plants, focusing on preventing shocks (insulated tools, ELCBs), falls (harnesses), and burns (fire-resistant clothing) to protect workers from electrical, height, and industrial hazards at sites like the Manjung Power Plant.

Since we needed a system to manage the purchasing and distribution of all these items, we developed and provided an inventory system called Inventory System PPEI. It includes the following features:

  1. Supplier Management — Manage supplier profiles, contact information, pricing agreements, and supply history; track supplier performance to ensure smooth procurement.
  2. Company Management — Maintain company profiles, branches, and organizational details; enable centralized management of multiple companies.
  3. Warehouse Management — Handle stock storage, inventory levels, inward/outward movements, and stock tracking for accurate inventory control.
  4. Product Management — Manage product categories, specifications, stock quantities, and product status for up-to-date product information.
  5. Department Management — Allocate products to departments and track department-wise usage and inventory distribution.
  6. Staff Management — Maintain staff profiles, roles, and assignments to track personnel involved in inventory handling.
  7. User Management — Control system access by managing user accounts, roles, and permissions to ensure data security and role-based access.
  8. Incoming Stocks — Record supplier information, purchase orders, quantities received, dates, and batch/reference numbers for accurate stock updates and visibility.
  9. Outgoing Stocks — Record department or staff allocations, quantities issued, dates, and approvals to track usage and maintain stock balances.

System Reports & Details

The system provides the following reports for monitoring, analysis, and decision-making:

  • Minimum Stock Level Report — This report displays products that have reached or fallen below the predefined minimum stock level. It helps administrators identify items that require immediate reordering to avoid stock shortages.
  • Inventory List — Provides a complete overview of all available inventory, including product names, quantities, warehouse locations, and current stock status.
  • Inventory List by Staff — Shows inventory items issued to specific staff members to track responsibility and usage.
  • Inventory List by Issue Date — Displays inventory records based on issue date to analyze stock movement over specific time periods.
  • Inventory List by Product Code — Allows users to view inventory details filtered by product code for quick lookup and tracking.
  • Product List Report — Detailed list of all products including product codes, categories, specifications, and status.
  • Stock Incoming Report — Shows details of incoming stock transactions, including supplier information, quantities received, and receiving dates.
  • Low Stock Report — Highlights products with low stock quantities approaching the minimum level to support proactive replenishment.
